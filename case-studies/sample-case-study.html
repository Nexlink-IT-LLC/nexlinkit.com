Case Study

Zero-downtime Nextcloud upgrade with performance tuning

How a remote team went from sluggish file access to a faster, hardened Nextcloud stack with clear rollback notes and verified backups.

Daniel Hammer
Owner, Nexlink IT LLC
Client: Distributed creative agency Platform: TrueNAS SCALE + Nextcloud 28 Timeline: 48 hours

How the engagement unfolded

The client was a distributed creative agency that lived inside Nextcloud every day. Uploads were sluggish, cron jobs failed, and TLS renewals were hit-or-miss. We agreed on a no-downtime upgrade with a rollback at every phase.

Before touching production, we verified ZFS snapshots and offsite backups, checked app compatibility, and wrote a two-step rollback. During a 48-hour window, we upgraded to Nextcloud 28, tuned Redis/MariaDB, renewed TLS with stricter HSTS/OCSP, and enabled OCSP stapling. Each change was logged with “what/why/rollback” notes the client could follow.

Validation mattered as much as delivery: we load-tested the busiest paths, trimmed app bottlenecks, and tightened alerting to signal only actionable issues. A concise runbook with screenshots and a two-step rollback guide shipped with the handoff.

What moved the needle

  • Backups verified before and after the upgrade; rollback tested in staging.
  • Redis and MariaDB tuned for concurrency; OPCache revalidation enabled.
  • TLS renewed with tighter HSTS and OCSP; alerts trimmed to essentials.
  • Runbook and rollback notes shipped with concise, stepwise commands.

Results: 38% faster page loads, no unplanned downtime, and a 24-hour turnaround from kick-off to handoff.
